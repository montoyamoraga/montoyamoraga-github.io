Aarón Montoya-Moraga is a Chilean artist, electrical engineer and educator who specializes in writing software for audiovisual interactive installations and live performances and media arts learning material in English and panish.

As a musical artist, he is part of Va la paloma, a noise multimedia duo based in Santiago de Chile, and a member of the Posternura artists collective. He performs solo as Víctor Raúl y sus Maquinitas, a one-man audiovisual band.

He has worked as a programmer and translator for the processing foundation, critter & guitari, dave & gabe and maría josé contreras lorenzini.

His work has been exhibited at World Maker Faire, NYC Computer Science Fair, alt-ai, Santiago Off.

He is currently a research resident at the Interactive Telecommunications Program, Tisch School of the Arts, New York University, where he graduated on a full scholarship from the Chilean Ministry of Education. He also is a co-director and teacher at CODED, a school for media arts based in Santiago de Chile, where he teaches free and open source software for media arts, such as Processing, p5.js, ChucC and Pure Data.
